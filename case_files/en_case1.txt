Dilemma 1: Hiring the first sales team member

You are a member of the sales team at Medilife, a start up company that develops technology based medical solutions. Following a successful pilot project, you have been asked to begin recruiting a new team.

At this stage, you need to choose the first employee who will join your team.

Management emphasized four central considerations that should be taken into account:
• Meeting sales targets of 1.2 million NIS per quarter
• Rapid implementation of a new customer relationship management system, CRM
• Maintaining a collaborative organizational culture
• Developing employees for the long term

Three candidates passed the screening stage:

Candidate A: An experienced salesperson with extensive experience in the medical field. He is considered a high performer, but tends to dominate conversations and does not always collaborate. According to reports, he may have received an offer from a competitor.

Candidate B: A candidate with about two years of experience, showing steady improvement and a desire to advance. She is considered friendly and has potential, but lacks experience with the central technology she will need to master.

Candidate C: Stable, quiet, and calm. He has experience from another field and learns quickly, but does not seek to stand out or compete.

Dilemma 2: Crisis with a strategic client

You are a team member in the customer service division of AeroFly, a global platform for booking private business flights. During an urgent team meeting, it was reported that one of the company's key clients, EuroSky, announced that it was ending the contract because service prices were too high. In practice, it appears that a new competitor is offering especially low prices.

This is a strategic client that contributes about seventeen percent of the company's quarterly revenue. The company CEO defined the situation as a strategic crisis and asked the team to propose an action plan within twenty four hours.

You have three possible courses of action, each with advantages and risks:

Option A: Offer the client an immediate discount of fifteen to twenty percent in order to try to retain it. This would require cutting existing budgets, and the move may create a precedent with additional clients.

Option B: Invest immediately in recruiting new clients in order to compensate for the loss. There is no prepared budget for this, and there is a risk that the move will require a long time and will not produce results in the short term.

Option C: Recommend cutting positions and staff in order to maintain budget stability. This action may harm service quality and team morale, especially given the planned onboarding of new employees in the near future.

Dilemma 3: Allocating a limited marketing budget

You are a team member in the marketing department of a natural cosmetics company that is launching a new product line for sensitive skin. The previous marketing manager recently left and left only partial documentation, so there is no clear basis for previous marketing decisions. The first campaign budget is limited due to an unexpected cut.

You need to recommend how to divide the budget among three marketing options. Each option requires investment and presents different advantages and disadvantages:

Option A: A paid campaign on the social media platforms Instagram and TikTok. Estimated cost: fifty thousand NIS. It enables reaching a young audience and broad exposure in a short time, but it is difficult to know how many people who view the ads actually buy the products. In the past, high engagement was reported, but it is unclear whether it led to actual purchases.

Option B: Paid advertising on search engines such as Google. Estimated cost: seventy thousand NIS. It enables reaching an audience searching for solutions for sensitive skin, but requires close monitoring and daily maintenance in order to prevent budget waste. There is no up to date information about how many people who are exposed to the advertising actually make a purchase.

Option C: A campaign based on collaborations with influencers in the health and cosmetics field. Estimated cost: ninety thousand NIS. It is difficult to measure direct impact, but collaborations of this type are considered effective for building reputation. The existing information about the success of similar campaigns is inconsistent.

Two senior team members presented opposing positions. One supports concentrating the budget in a measurable and controlled channel. The other prefers splitting the investment among the three options in order to spread risk. The decision is now in your hands.

Dilemma 4: Team conflict

You are a team member in the human resources function of a high tech company operating in a competitive environment. As part of your role, you support the development team, which has grown significantly over the past year and has entered a critical project for a new version of the company's core product.

During support and follow up conversations, an ongoing conflict emerged between two veteran employees on the team, Rona and Lior. The conflict is negatively affecting both the team atmosphere and the pace of progress on the project.

Rona is a senior programmer, empathic, and has strong systemic understanding. She is valued by the younger team members, but tends to get hurt easily and avoids confrontations. In a personal conversation, she said she cannot work when she feels she is being treated in a condescending way.

Lior is a veteran engineer with high technical ability. He is direct and sometimes blunt. Under pressure, he tends to take command, but sometimes excludes other employees from the process. In an internal conversation, he said he has no patience for "sensitivities" and that whoever cannot keep up is probably not suitable.

In addition, younger employees on the team reported a sense of factions and said they do not feel safe expressing their opinions. One of them even asked to move to another team if the situation does not change.

As part of the human resources team supporting the team, you are required to recommend a course of action. There are three options:

Option A: Recommend that the team manager initiate an open conversation with Rona and Lior, accompanied by you as the human resources representative. There is a risk that the conversation will develop into a confrontation, but if it succeeds, it may improve the team climate and prevent employees from leaving.

Option B: Recommend functional separation between them, meaning assigning them to different projects or separate work groups. This action may reduce tension in the short term, but may be perceived as giving up on direct confrontation and may harm the team's existing structure.

Option C: Wait before direct intervention and continue collecting information through conversations with additional team members. This approach allows building a more accurate picture, but may worsen employees' frustration and lead the situation to deteriorate.
